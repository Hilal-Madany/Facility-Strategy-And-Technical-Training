# 🏥 Universal SOP: Medical Gas Pipeline Systems (MGPS) Operations
> **Standards Alignment:** CBAHI (Saudi Arabia), JCI (Global), & HTM 02-01
> **Category:** Healthcare Facility Management & Life-Critical Infrastructure

## 1. Executive Summary
Medical Gas Pipeline Systems (MGPS) are classified as **Life-Support Systems**. This SOP outlines the daily, weekly, and monthly protocols required to ensure 100% uptime and regulatory compliance in a clinical environment.

---

## 2. Technical Scope & Inventory
The MGPS must manage and monitor the following essential services:
* **Medical Oxygen (O₂):** Primary Liquid (LOX) and Secondary/Reserve Manifolds.
* **Medical Air (4-Bar):** For patient ventilation and respiratory therapy.
* **Surgical Air (7-Bar):** For pneumatic surgical tool operation.
* **Medical Vacuum:** High-flow suction for theaters and wards.
* **AGSS:** Anesthesia Gas Scavenging Systems for waste gas removal.
* **Specialty Gases:** N₂O, CO₂, and Entonox (O₂/N₂O mixture).



---

## 3. Daily Operational Protocols (The Safety Rounds)
As per **CBAHI** and **FM Best Practices**, the following checks are non-negotiable:

| System | Daily Inspection Task | Warning Signs |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bulk LOX Tank** | Verify liquid level and tank pressure. | Heavy icing on vaporizers; low DOT pressure. |
| **Manifolds** | Check "Duty" bank status and "Standby" pressure. | Empty cylinders on the reserve bank. |
| **Plant Rooms** | Monitor Compressor/Vacuum pump oil levels. | Unusual vibration, high temp, or oil leaks. |
| **Alarms** | Test Local and Master Alarm Panels. | "Low Pressure" or "Fault" lights ignored. |

---

## 4. Planned Preventive Maintenance (PPM) Schedule
FM Companies must adhere to a strict maintenance frequency to prevent catastrophic failure:

### **Monthly (Technical Check)**
* **Compressors:** Check belt tension, clean intake filters, and log loading/unloading hours.
* **Dryers:** Verify Dew Point ($<-46°C$ for Medical Air) and automatic drain functionality.
* **Vacuum:** Clean bacterial filters and check pump cooling fans.

### **Quarterly (Clinical Check)**
* **Terminal Units:** Test "Tug" and "Flow" for all wall outlets (O₂, Vac, Air).
* **Area Valve Service Units (AVSU):** Ensure valves operate smoothly and isolate zones correctly.



---

## 5. Cleanliness & Infection Control (Hygienic Maintenance)
Healthcare FM differs from Industrial FM in its focus on **Aseptic Environments**:
* **Plant Rooms:** Must be dust-free to prevent intake contamination in compressors.
* **Cylinder Storage:** Organized by gas type, clearly labeled "Full" or "Empty," and secured with safety chains.
* **LOX Perimeter:** Keep 3 meters clear of any combustible materials (oil, grease, or vegetation).

---

## 6. Documentation & Reporting (CBAHI Compliance)
For audit readiness, every FM team must maintain:
1. **Daily Log Sheets:** Pressure readings and manifold status.
2. **PPM Certificates:** Signed by the Engineer and verified by the Hospital Safety Officer.
3. **Consumption Analytics:** Tracking Liquid Oxygen and Cylinder turnover to manage supply chain risks.
4. **Achievement Log:** Documenting every user complaint resolved and leak repaired.

---

## 7. Emergency Response
In the event of a system failure (e.g., Oxygen low pressure):
1. **Notify:** Immediate alert to Nursing Staff and Clinical Engineering.
2. **Isolate:** Use AVSUs to contain leaks if applicable.
3. **Deploy:** Switch to the Emergency Reserve Manifold (ERM) or portable cylinders.
